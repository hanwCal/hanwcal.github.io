MLDM Lab

Mathematical & Learning-based Dynamics for Mobility

Traffic Flow Modeling | Mixed Autonomy | Intelligent Transportation Systems

Welcome to the MLDM Lab in the Department of Civil and Environmental Engineering at UC Berkeley. Our research lies at the intersection of transportation engineering, mathematics, and control theory.

We focus on understanding how technology impacts transportation systems - particularly how connected and automated vehicles affect traffic flow, safety, and sustainability. Our work combines mathematical modeling with large-scale field experiments to develop practical solutions for real-world traffic challenges.

Our Research

CIRCLES: 100-Vehicle Open-Road Traffic Control Experiment

Congestion Impacts Reduction via CAV-in-the-loop Lagrangian Energy Smoothing

The largest open-road traffic control experiment to date. In November 2022, we deployed 100 AI-equipped vehicles (Nissan Rogue, Toyota RAV4, Cadillac XT5) on I-24 near Nashville, Tennessee. The MegaVanderTest demonstrated that even small proportions of connected automated vehicles can significantly improve traffic flow.

Key Results: 8% overall traffic density decrease, 52% reduction during congestion formation phase, validating the potential of Lagrangian traffic control.

PORA: Black-box Safety Evaluation of Autonomous Vehicles

Probabilistic Occupancy Risk Assessment via Monte Carlo Simulation

A vendor-agnostic framework for third-party AV safety assessment. Using probabilistic occupancy heatmaps and Monte Carlo simulation, this framework enables insurance companies, regulators, and certification bodies to objectively evaluate AV safety without accessing proprietary controller internals.

Key Innovation: PORA metric provides superior predictive power over traditional TTC measures, with smooth monotonic risk gradients validated on Argoverse 2 dataset.
