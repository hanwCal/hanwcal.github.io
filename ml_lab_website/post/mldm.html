Prof. Maria Laura Delle Monache

Assistant Professor | Clare and Hsieh Wen Shen Endowed Distinguished Professor

Maria Laura Delle Monache is an Assistant Professor and Clare and Hsieh Wen Shen Endowed Distinguished Professor in the Department of Civil and Environmental Engineering at the University of California, Berkeley. She joined UC Berkeley's faculty on July 1, 2021.

Her research lies at the intersection of transportation engineering, mathematics, and control theory, with a focus on understanding the implications of technology on transportation systems - particularly how technology affects traffic, safety, and sustainability.

Research Interests

  • Traffic Flow Theory and Modeling: Mathematical modeling using conservation laws and hyperbolic PDEs, coupled PDE-ODE systems for mixed traffic scenarios
  • Mixed Autonomy Traffic Control: Lagrangian control using autonomous vehicles as mobile actuators, reinforcement learning-based adaptive speed controllers
  • Numerical Methods: Finite volume algorithms for constrained hyperbolic PDEs, front tracking and wave front methods
  • Energy and Sustainability: Fuel consumption optimization through traffic smoothing, air quality benefits from autonomous vehicle intervention
  • Cyber-Physical Transportation Systems: Real-time traffic state estimation, vehicle-infrastructure integration, large-scale field experiments
