Black-box Safety Evaluation of Autonomous Vehicles via Monte Carlo Simulation

A vendor-agnostic framework for third-party AV safety assessment using probabilistic occupancy prediction

Ensuring the safety of autonomous vehicles (AVs) requires rigorous evaluation before deployment. Traditional methods either require access to internal controller details (white-box) or use deterministic surrogates that under-represent real-world complexity. We present a fully black-box framework that enables third-party entities (insurance companies, government agencies, regulators) to objectively evaluate and compare AV safety without accessing internal algorithms, parameters, or decision logic.

Three-Module Architecture

Our framework uses a modular, implementation-agnostic architecture consisting of three interchangeable components:

Module 1: Heatmap Predictor - Generates probabilistic occupancy predictions for future agent positions using a Transformer-based encoder and GAN-based decoder. Outputs probability distributions over a 200m x 200m grid with 3-second prediction horizon.

Module 2: Goal Sampler - Converts probabilistic heatmaps into discrete goal points using various strategies: pure-probability sampling, maximum-probability sampling, non-maximum suppression (NMS), edge-case sampling for stress-testing, and density-based sampling.

Module 3: Trajectory Connector - Converts sampled goals into continuous, dynamically feasible trajectories using a neural network trained on real-world driving sequences.

Monte Carlo Simulation Framework

The simulation framework integrates real-world trajectory data from the Argoverse 2 dataset (11,000+ scenarios from 6 U.S. cities) and road geometry to create realistic traffic scenarios. We use SUMO for microscopic traffic simulation and run Monte Carlo trials to quantify safety metrics statistically.

Probabilistic Occupancy Risk Assessment (PORA)

We introduce PORA as a novel surrogate safety measure that combines collision probability with Time-to-Collision severity. Unlike traditional TTC metrics, PORA:

  • Uses full probabilistic occupancy heatmaps over future positions
  • Captures both spatial and temporal uncertainties
  • Produces smooth, monotonic risk gradients
  • Demonstrates superior predictive power and reliability

Key Results

Our framework successfully captures causal effects of degraded sensing and slower reaction times. Key findings include:

  • Clear monotonic risk gradient: Collision risk increases smoothly with larger reaction delay and smaller perception range
  • Tail risk amplification: 80th percentile scenarios show 1-2 orders of magnitude higher collision rates than median
  • Optimal configuration: HM(1,1) with NMS_6 sampler achieves 94% reduction in spurious incidents compared to naive sampling
  • Controller benchmarking: Framework successfully differentiates between controller designs (RL-based vs. sample-based)

Applications & Impact

This black-box evaluation framework enables:

  • Insurance Companies: Objective risk assessment without proprietary access
  • Government Agencies: Regulatory compliance verification
  • Industry Consortia: Standardized benchmarking across manufacturers
  • Certification Bodies: Transparent safety certification

By providing a vendor-agnostic evaluation pathway, this framework contributes to safer transportation systems and accelerates responsible AV deployment.
