Some AR / VR Related Personal Hobby Projects

I'm a big fan of AR/VR technologies and have been working on some personal hobby projects. Here are some of them. AR-based Visual Perception Assistance System

Driving technology has improved road safety, yet visually impaired drivers face unique challenges. To address this, our team at UC Berkeley developed an AR-based driving assistance system. This system uses computer vision and AR to identify and display traffic signals and signs through an intuitive AR interface.

The development and testing were conducted in a VR simulation environment. This allowed for controlled evaluation and refinement, ensuring the system's potential to aid visually impaired drivers, particularly those with color vision deficiencies.

The system architecture includes:

  • VR Simulation Environment: Developed using Unity, it simulates city driving scenarios for safe and controlled testing.
  • Object Recognition Model: Utilizes the YOLOv8 model for real-time object detection, fine-tuned on a custom dataset of traffic lights.
  • AR Indicator Rendering: Provides dynamic visual cues for traffic signals in the driver's line of sight.

Preliminary tests indicated the system's potential to improve traffic signal recognition for colorblind drivers. The system's capabilities and limitations were discussed, highlighting the need for real-world implementation and testing to address challenges such as object recognition precision and hardware limitations.

Future work involves implementing the system on AR devices and conducting extensive real-world testing. This is crucial for validating the system's effectiveness and addressing the legal and ethical considerations of using AR technology in driving.
